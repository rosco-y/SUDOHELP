What is a SudoKube?

A SudoKube consists of 27 Sudoku Puzzles meshed together to form a 9x9x9 Cube made of Sudoku Puzzles.

27 Sudoku Puzzles, One Layer at a Time.

At first blush, this might look simply like 27 random Sudoku puzzles pasted into a cube, but care must be taken to observe the traditional Rules of Sudoku so that 1-9 is maintained in all 3 directions: Back-to-Front, Right-to-Left and Top-toBottom.  That is, each column and row, height, width or depth must contain the values 1-9 inclusive and with no omissions.

Note: It should be noted that the although Sudoku Cells represent a puzzle's solution, they are not unique to a given Layer, or Puzzle.  Each cell shares itself with 3 puzzles, --the one facing Front, the facing Up, and the one facing Right.  At a glance, it may look like a Cell could contain multiple values, depending on your view of it: FRONT, TOP or RIGHT.  In fact, each cell contains only one solution-value.  As a result, when you solve a value for a Cell, you are actually solving the Cell for 3 puzzles at once.

Also notice that each 3x3 region in all of the layers must also contain 1-9 without omissions.
