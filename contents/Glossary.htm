Glossary

There are some terms used throughout the help system that it may be helpful to define:

  1. Candidate

    A Candidate is one possible solution for an unsolved cell in a Suduko puzzle.  Until a puzzle is solved there are many situations where a cell may have multiple candidates.  When a value is unknown, a solution might be multiple values (e.g, "this cell may be a 7, 9, 5 or a 6, I just don't exactly know yet.")

  2. SudoKube

    A SudoKube is a collection of 27 Sudoku puzzles; 9, front-to-back; 9 side-to-side and 9 top-to-bottom. They are layered together in such a way that they form a 3 dimensional cube, remeniscent of the popular Rubik's Cube. SudoKube

  4. Cell
    Cells are placeholders for all of the 81 (9x9( Values in a Sudoku Puzzle.  In SudoKube, cells are represented as Cubes, either blank (needing to be solved) or with an initial value (a given.)
  5. Region
    A Region is a 3x3 subgrid of a Sudoku puzzle.  When the puzzle is completed, each Region contains the Values 1-9 with no omissions.  There are 9 regions in a Sudoku Puzzle, they are ususally set apart with extra space between the regions.

  6. Holes
    The term "Holes" refers to cells with no initial value presented to you, the puzzle solver.  The object of Sudoku puzzles is to fill in the holes.
